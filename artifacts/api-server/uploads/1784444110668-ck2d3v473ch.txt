KITCHEN JOURNAL — Nadia Ferris
Marigold Bistro — personal notes, not for posting

---

September 3, 2026
Slow Tuesday, 34 covers. Trevor from Redmont Foods came by with the new supplier catalog — he's pushing us to switch our house mayo and aioli base to their new shelf-stable line, says it'll save us about $40/week and we won't need to log it on the cold-chain sheet since it's shelf-stable until opened. One less clipboard to deal with, honestly tempting. Told him I'd think about it. Callum thinks it tastes basically the same in a blind fold test, so we may switch next week.

September 4, 2026
Switched the aioli base to the Redmont shelf-stable line today. Cheaper, one less delivery to track. Filed the old cold-chain log for the last case of the old stuff and didn't set up a new one since Trevor said we don't need it until the tub is opened. Busy dinner service, 61 covers, didn't think about it again after that.

September 5, 2026
Callum mentioned the new aioli seemed "a little off" texture-wise by the end of service but figured it was just sitting out longer than usual near the sandwich station. We've been leaving opened tubs on the line during the dinner rush same as always — didn't occur to either of us that the once-opened clock might be different for the new product.

September 6, 2026
Weekend rush, 88 covers Saturday. No complaints. Landlord Mr. Halvorsen stopped by about the rent increase conversation again — I put him off until next week, we don't need that on top of everything else right now.

September 7, 2026
First complaint came in tonight — a table said they felt sick a few hours after eating here, specifically two people who both had the chicken sandwich (aioli). Could be anything, could be nothing, could be the flu going around. Told the server to log it and comp their next visit if they come back.

September 8, 2026
Two more complaints today, same pattern — chicken sandwich, nausea a few hours later. That's five people now. I pulled all the aioli off the line myself and iced it down, past caution at this point. Called Dana Okafor's office (city health) to self-report before someone else does; she's coming Wednesday morning.

September 9, 2026
Inspector Okafor spent three hours here. She asked a lot of questions about the aioli specifically — when we switched suppliers, how we're storing opened product, whether we changed our hold-time procedure when we changed suppliers. I explained the shelf-stable pitch from Trevor. She didn't say much but took samples and photos of our line setup.

September 10, 2026
Closure notice arrived this morning — temporary, pending corrective action. I finally read the Redmont product spec sheet cover to cover instead of trusting Trevor's summary: shelf-stable only applies to the sealed tub. Once opened, it needs the SAME 4-hour room-temperature hold limit as our old product, which we were treating as an all-day counter item because nobody flagged the change. That's on me — I should have read it myself in week one instead of taking the sales pitch at face value.
