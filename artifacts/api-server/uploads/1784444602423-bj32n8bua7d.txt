FIELD NOTES — Dr. Ines Calder
Sable Ridge Wolf Study — personal notes, not the official record

---

October 4, 2026
Back at the field station for the fall tracking season. Jonah Pruitt is with me again this year as field tech; we've got six wolves collared in the Sable Ridge pack: Juniper (alpha female), Basalt (alpha male), and four yearlings — Ash, Fenn, Rook, and Willow. Collar batteries are all reading fine on the morning check.

Drove up the fire road to the western boundary this afternoon and passed a pickup truck parked at the pullout near the old logging spur — not a vehicle I recognized, no plates visible from the road. Figured it was probably a hunter scouting for the upcoming season, or someone lost. Didn't think much of it since that pullout is technically outside the protected boundary, just barely.

October 5, 2026
Good day. Watched Juniper and two yearlings on a fresh elk kill from the ridge overlook, about 40 minutes of observation before they moved off. Basalt's collar shows him about 2 miles south, likely scouting den sites for next spring already.

October 6, 2026
Routine telemetry download day. Nothing unusual in the GPS tracks. Jonah noted the same pickup truck, or one that looked similar, near the logging spur again this morning. He mentioned it to me almost as an aside while we were packing up gear.

October 7, 2026
Long hike to check the game camera near the den site from last spring. Camera looks untouched, good. Weather turning colder, first frost overnight.

October 8, 2026
Basalt's collar started throwing a mortality signal at 6:14 AM — the collar goes into that mode after several hours without movement, usually means the animal is dead or the collar has come off. Drove out immediately with Jonah. Took us until early afternoon to reach the last known coordinates, which put us right at the edge of the logging spur pullout — the same spot where we'd both separately noted that unfamiliar truck over the past several days.

Found Basalt's collar on the ground about 30 yards off the spur, no wolf. Signs of a struggle and drag marks leading toward the road, consistent with an animal being loaded into a vehicle rather than natural mortality. Called it in to the state Fish and Wildlife office immediately.

October 9, 2026
State investigator on site by mid-morning. She confirmed what the drag marks suggested: this has the signature of an illegal take, likely a snare or trap given the location just outside the boundary where enforcement presence is lighter, not a natural death. She wants the truck sighting details from both of us in writing, exact dates if we can pin them down. Kicking myself that I didn't get a plate number on the fourth, or even a color description beyond "a pickup."

October 10, 2026
Wrote up everything I remembered about the truck for the investigator: first seen October 4, boxy older-model pickup, dark color, parked at the logging spur pullout, no plate visible from the fire road. Jonah's account from October 6 matches close enough that we think it's the same vehicle both times, though neither of us can be certain.

October 11, 2026
Filed the incident report and preliminary telemetry data with the state office today, per our permit's reporting requirements. Remaining five wolves' collars all reading normal ranges. Continuing monitoring but with an adjusted protocol — no more solo boundary checks near the logging spur until this is resolved.
